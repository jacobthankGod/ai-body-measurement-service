# KORRA: Strict-Scan Clinical Validity Policy (Phase 75)
**Perspective:** Chief Medical Compliance Officer & Lead AI Architect
**Status:** ACTIVE

---

### **1. The Single Source of Truth (SSOT)**
Every 3D Digital Twin and biometric report generated by KORRA must be derived exclusively from a verified AI scan.

### **2. Prohibition of Manual Overrides**
*   **Manual Input Blocking:** All manual biometric input fields in the user dashboard are disabled.
*   **API Integrity:** The `POST /api/v2/refinement/impute` and mesh synthesis endpoints reject any data not flagged with `source_of_truth: true`.
*   **Database Immutability:** Once a scan is finalized, the `biometrics` JSONB column is locked via the `trigger_strict_scan_integrity` SQL trigger. Any attempt to modify measurements via the UI or external API will be terminated with a `Clinical Policy Exception`.

### **3. Validation Thresholds**
*   **Confidence Guard:** Scans with a MediaPipe/HMR confidence score below **85%** are automatically rejected and will not trigger 3D reshaping.
*   **3-Sigma Rule:** Any scanned measurement falling more than **3 standard deviations** outside the ANSUR II clinical mean for the user's gender/height profile will be flagged for "Surgical Recalibration" or re-scan.

### **4. Skeletal & Rigging Parity**
*   **Joint Recalibration:** To prevent "rig snapping" (skeletal misalignment), joint centers are dynamically recalculated as the centroid of the reshaped vertex groups (Phase 68).
*   **Volume Integrity:** The "Volume Guard" prevents mesh deformation beyond anatomically safe limits ($0.7x$ to $1.6x$ expansion).

---

**Execution:** This policy is enforced at the database level (RLS/Triggers), the service level (Python OOM Guards), and the UI level (Disabled Inputs).
