# KORRA: Remote Measurement & Artisan Empowerment Plan
**Core Pivot**: Moving from "Scanner Cost" to "Remote Measurement Friction" (The Real LMIC Bottleneck).

---

## 1. The Validated Problem: The "WhatsApp Guessing Game"
In emerging markets, the industrial bottleneck isn't just the price of a scanner—it's the physical distance between the client and the tailor.

### The "Remote Friction" Audit:
*   **The Diaspora/Migration Reality**: Tailors in Lagos/Mumbai/Accra often serve clients in different cities or abroad (UK/US).
*   **The Data Failure**: Clients send self-reported measurements via text or say "use my old size," leading to garments that are too tight, too short, or unwearable.
*   **The Financial Drain**: This causes multiple remakes, wasted expensive fabric, delayed deliveries, and destroyed trust.

---

## 2. High-Impact Refactoring Strategy

### A. The "Status Quo" Section (The Pivot)
*   **New Narrative**: "The Guessing Game of Remote Measurement."
*   **Focus**: Capturing the frustration of tailors receiving inaccurate self-reports from distant clients.

### B. The "Cross-Border" Section (Global Diaspora Focus)
*   **Visual**: Explicitly mention the **"London Client. Lagos Tailor."** use case.
*   **Messaging**: "Distance-Free Precision. Your client scans in one city; you build in another. Identical data, perfect fit."

### C. The Individual User Section (The Passport)
*   **Hook**: "Measure Once. Share Anywhere."
*   **Benefit**: Stop visiting the shop for every fitting. Own your digital size profile and share it with your favorite artisans via a simple link or ID.

### D. DIV Fund Alignment: "Artisan Economic Empowerment"
*   **Value Case**: KORRA empowers local craftsmen to tap into global markets by removing the "Fittings Barrier."
