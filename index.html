The Problem

Manual measuring slows everything down.

Taking measurements by hand takes time. It also creates mistakes, extra fittings, and more work. The bigger problem is that measurements are often not the same from one person to another. A customer can get different numbers depending on who measures them.

INDUSTRIAL ERROR VISUALIZATION
The Frustration Loop

Common problems.

For many people, there is no easy way to keep a reliable size record. This creates a cycle of failed expectations.

Fit still goes wrong

Even when measurements are provided, garments frequently come back too long, too small, or too tight — and disputes over measurement error add to the frustration.

Delayed delivery

Tailors and customers have to commit to a date and time for trial fittings, and the timeline frequently exceeds what was agreed. Multiple revision rounds compound this.

Sizing has no standard

62% of consumers struggle to find clothing that fits, largely due to a lack of standardization — and even custom tailoring doesn't fully solve this when measurements are collected inconsistently.

The Solution
