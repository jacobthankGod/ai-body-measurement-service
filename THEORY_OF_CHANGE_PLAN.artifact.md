# Theory of Change - Implementation Plan

This document outlines the content structure for `theoryofchange.html` to align with KORRA's industrial and economic impact messaging.

## The Logic Model

### 1. The Input (Technology)
*   Smartphone-based 3D Human Mesh Recovery (HMR).
*   Clinical-grade biometric extraction ($0.50/scan).
*   Global Identity Layer (Size Passport).

### 2. The Output (Efficiency)
*   Replacement of manual measuring with digital accuracy.
*   Standardization of sizing across artisans and borders.
*   Zero-latency sharing of fit data.

### 3. The Outcome (Economic Multiplier)
*   **For Artisans**: 90% reduction in remakes $\rightarrow$ higher profit margins $\rightarrow$ ability to scale.
*   **For Clients**: 100% fit confidence $\rightarrow$ reduced frustration $\rightarrow$ ownership of biometric data.
*   **For the Industry**: Reduction in the 40% return rate for online apparel.

### 4. The Impact (Global Development)
*   **Market Access**: Tailors in LMICs serving the global diaspora.
*   **Sustainability**: Massive reduction in textile waste from "trial and error" tailoring.
*   **Digital Inclusion**: Democratizing Savile Row-level fit via basic smartphones.

- **Section 2: The Solution**: The 3D Digital Twin as a universal standard.
- **Section 3: The Multiplier**: Comparison of "Traditional Tailoring" vs "KORRA-Powered Tailoring" unit economics.
