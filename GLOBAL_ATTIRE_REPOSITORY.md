# KORRA Global Attire Repository (Pan-African & Global Edition)
**Objective:** A comprehensive database of global garment types to drive "Tolerance Intelligence" offsets for tailored fit, with a specific focus on Pan-African coverage and LMIC Inclusion.

---

## 1. West Africa (The "Grandeur" Archetypes)
*   **Agbada / Babban Riga (Nigeria/Niger):** Massive three-piece robe; requires extreme chest and span ease (+20cm to +30cm).
*   **Buba & Iro (Nigeria/Benin):** Blouse and wrapper set; requires specific "Wrap-over" width calculations.
*   **Senator Style (Nigeria):** Sharp-cut two-piece cashmere/wool ensemble; requires moderate "Business" ease (+4cm to +6cm).
*   **Kente (Ghana/Ivory Coast):** Hand-woven silk/cotton wrapped like a toga; requires "Masculine Drape" volume offsets.
*   **Fugu / Smock (Ghana/Burkina Faso):** Heavy, hand-woven cotton tunic; requires "Structured Flare" allowance.
*   **Grand Boubou (Senegal/Mali/Guinea):** Voluminous, embroidered robe made of polished cotton (Bazin); requires maximum sleeve-span ease.
*   **Etibo (Nigeria - Niger Delta):** South-South long shirt worn with a wrapper; requires precise "Length-to-Hip" ease.
*   **Dashiki:** V-neck loose tunic; requires significant armhole and chest ease.
*   **Faso Dan Fani (Burkina Faso):** National hand-woven cotton; requires "Textile Stiffness" compensation.
*   **Lappa / Buppa (Sierra Leone/Liberia):** Wrap skirt and blouse; regional "Hip Volume" preference.

## 2. East Africa & The Horn (The "Ethereal & Draped" Archetypes)
*   **Habesha Kemis (Ethiopia/Eritrea):** Hand-woven white cotton dress with Tibeb embroidery; requires "Waist-Cinch" vs. "Full-Length" drape offsets.
*   **Kanzu (Kenya/Tanzania/Uganda):** White or cream ankle-length Swahili robe; requires cylindrical "Vertical Drop" ease.
*   **Gomesi / Busuti (Uganda):** High-structure dress with exaggerated shoulders; requires "Shoulder-to-Base" vertical ease and "Waist Sash" volume.
*   **Dirac (Somalia/Djibouti):** Sheer, light-weight long dress; requires "Fluid Over-slip" allowance.
*   **Guntiino (Somalia):** Sari-like wrap; requires "Diagonal Torso Span" measurements.
*   **Maasai Shúkà (Kenya/Tanzania):** Red checkered wrap; requires "Body-to-Fabric Wrap Ratio."
*   **Kanga / Leso:** Printed Swahili wraps; biometric context for "Wrap Overlap" width.
*   **Mushanana (Rwanda/Burundi):** Sash-like wrap over floor-length skirt; requires "Diagonal Drape" precision.

## 3. North Africa & The Maghreb (The "Modesty & Arid" Archetypes)
*   **Djellaba (Morocco/Algeria):** Hooded unisex robe; requires "Shoulder-to-Hood" and "Desert Ventilation" ease.
*   **Takshita (Morocco):** Two-piece formal dress with heavy belt; requires "Corset-Cinched Waist" vs. "Voluminous Caftan" contrast.
*   **Karakou (Algeria):** Fitted velvet jacket with gold embroidery; requires high-precision "Back S-Curve" ease.
*   **Jalabiya (Egypt/Libya/Sudan):** Ankle-length, wide-sleeved robe; requires "Maximum Airflow" cylindrical ease.
*   **Burnous (The Maghreb):** Hooded woolen cloak; requires "Shoulder Span" focus.
*   **Melhfa (Mauritania/Western Sahara):** Voluminous 6-yard wrap; requires "Maximum Surface Area" drape math.

## 4. Southern Africa (The "Symbolic & Structured" Archetypes)
*   **Umbhaco (South Africa - Xhosa):** Heavy cotton skirt with braided lines; requires "Textile Weight" and "Hip Girth" tolerance.
*   **Herero Dress (Namibia):** Victorian-style gown with "Cow Horn" hat; requires extreme "Petticoat Volume" and high-neck ease.
*   **Basotho Blanket (Lesotho):** Woolen cloak; requires "Shoulder-to-Calf" drape volume.
*   **Leteise / Shweshwe (Botswana):** Indigo-dyed tailored dresses; requires "Stiff Cotton Shell" ease.
*   **Emahiya (Eswatini):** Cloth wraps tied at the shoulder; requires "Knot-Point" biometric context.
*   **Isicholo & Isidwaba (South Africa - Zulu):** Traditional hat and leather skirt; head and hip biometric constraints.
*   **Lamba (Madagascar):** Silk/Raffia wrap; requires "Multi-function Wrap" volume.

## 5. Central Africa (The "Regal & Textured" Archetypes)
*   **Toghu (Cameroon):** Embroidered velvet robe; heavy fabric requires "Expansion Tolerance" and "Structured Torso" offsets.
*   **Liputa (DRC/Congo):** Vibrant wax-print ensembles; requires "Pagne Wrapper" length precision.
*   **Muhuila Beaded Gear (Angola):** Heavy beaded collars; requires "Neck-Shoulder Load" biometric data.

## 6. Global & Industrial (The Functional Archetypes)
*   **Bespoke Suit (Savile Row):** Precision construction; requires "Structured Shoulder" ease.
*   **Slim Fit / Italian Cut:** Minimalist ease; focuses on the "S-Curve" of the back.
*   **Hanbok (Korea):** High-waisted wrap skirt (Chima); requires specific "Bust-to-Waist" drape ease.
*   **Ao Dai (Vietnam):** Tight tunic over trousers; requires hyper-precise waist-to-hip transitions.
*   **Kimono (Japan):** T-shaped wrap; requires "Flat Panel" conversion from round measurements.
*   **Guayabera (Latin America):** Pleated summer shirt; requires "Heat-Ventilation" ease in the back.
*   **Bombachas (Gaucho):** Wide-legged trousers; requires extreme "Thigh Volume" ease.
*   **Activewear / Leggings:** Stretch-based; requires **Negative Ease** (smaller than body).
*   **Corporate Uniforms:** Standardized ease for mass-logistics.

---
**Data Policy:** All attires are mapped to a "Tolerance Multiplier" (e.g., Grand Boubou = 1.4x Girth) to be provided as a data stream alongside the raw KORRA biometric scan.
